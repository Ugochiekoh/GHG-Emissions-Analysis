# Fast Cars GHG Emissions Analysis Dashboard

## Overview

This project was developed as an extension of the BCG Climate & Sustainability Job Simulation on Forage. While the simulation focused on carbon emissions analysis, climate strategy recommendations, and stakeholder communication, I decided to take the learning a step further by building an interactive Power BI dashboard to visualize and analyze the emissions data provided in the case study.

## Objectives

The dashboard was designed to:

- Analyze greenhouse gas (GHG) emissions across Scope 1, Scope 2, and Scope 3 categories
- Identify the major contributors to the company's carbon footprint
- Highlight emissions hotspots that could inform reduction strategies
- Transform raw sustainability data into actionable business insights

## Dashboard Features

- KPI cards showing total emissions and emissions by scope
- Emissions breakdown by Scope 1, Scope 2, and Scope 3
- Scope 3 emissions analysis by category
- Emissions trends across reporting years
- Key insights section summarizing findings and implications

## Key Findings

- Scope 3 emissions accounted for approximately 88% of total emissions.
- The use of sold products was the largest source of emissions.
- Purchased goods and services represented another significant emissions hotspot.
- Direct and indirect energy emissions accounted for a relatively small share of total emissions.

These findings demonstrate the importance of addressing emissions across the value chain, as organizations seeking to achieve Net Zero often face their greatest emissions challenges outside their direct operations.
